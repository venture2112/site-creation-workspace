Why Implants?

Replacement That Preserves Your Jawbone

When you lose a tooth, the surrounding bone begins to resorb — shrink away — over time. Bridges and dentures sit on top of the gums but don't stimulate the bone. Implants do.

A dental implant is a small titanium post surgically placed into your jawbone. Over several months, the bone fuses to the post (osseointegration), creating an anchor as stable as a natural tooth root. A custom crown is then attached on top.
