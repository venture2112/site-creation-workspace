What Is a Dental Crown?

Full Coverage. Natural Look. Lasting Protection.

When a tooth is too damaged for a filling alone — weakened by decay, a large filling, or a root canal — a crown is the solution. A crown is a custom-made cap that covers the entire visible portion of the tooth, restoring its shape, size, and function.

When Is a Crown Needed?
  • Teeth weakened by large fillings or root canals
  • Cracked or fractured teeth
  • Cosmetic improvement for severely discolored or misshapen teeth
  • Covering a dental implant
Two Visits, One Smile

Your Crown Procedure

We complete the entire process in just two comfortable appointments — here's what to expect.

1 Visit 1 — Preparation

Shaping & Impressions

Your dentist reshapes the tooth to prepare it for the crown, takes digital impressions for a precise fit, and fits a temporary crown to protect your tooth while the permanent one is being crafted.

2 Visit 2 — Placement

Custom Fit & Cementation

The temporary crown is removed and your custom crown is carefully checked for fit, color, and bite alignment. Once everything is perfect, it is cemented securely in place — your tooth is restored.

Crown Materials

Choose the Right Material for You

We offer a range of crown materials to match your needs — from natural-looking ceramics to ultra-durable gold alloys.

Material Best For Appearance
Porcelain/Ceramic Front teeth Most natural look
Porcelain-fused-to-metal Any tooth Natural with metal strength
Zirconia Molars Extremely strong, tooth-colored
Gold alloy Back molars Durable, minimal tooth removal

Longevity: With good hygiene, crowns last 10–15 years or longer. Treat them like natural teeth — brush, floss, and keep up with regular checkups.
